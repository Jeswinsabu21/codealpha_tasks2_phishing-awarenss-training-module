What is Phishing?

Phishing is a form of cyber attack where attackers impersonate legitimate organizations to steal sensitive information.

Types of Phishing

Email Phishing

Fake emails that seem to come from trusted organizations.

Website Spoofing

Fake websites that impersonate real ones to steal login credentials.

Social Engineering

Manipulating individuals into revealing confidential information.

Social Engineering

Social engineering is when attackers manipulate you into revealing sensitive information.

  • Pretexting: Attacker fabricates a story to get your info.
  • Baiting: Offering something enticing to get you to click or share info.
  • Tailgating: Gaining physical access by following someone in.

Email Phishing

Phishing emails trick you into sharing personal information.

  • Suspicious sender address: Look for slight differences in email addresses.
  • Urgent requests: Phishing emails often create a sense of urgency.
  • Malicious links: Hover over links to check their validity before clicking.

Website Spoofing

Fake websites that mimic real sites to steal personal information.

  • URL mismatch: Check the domain name and ensure it's the real one.
  • Design differences: Fake sites often have small design errors.
  • Unwanted pop-ups: Be cautious of sites that ask you to download files.

Red Flags to Look For

  • Suspicious or unfamiliar sender email addresses.
  • Urgent or threatening language in messages.
  • Broken links or incorrect URLs.
  • Unexpected attachments or downloads.

Test Your Knowledge: Phishing or Legit?

Scenario: You receive an email from a bank asking you to reset your password via a suspicious link.




Conclusion & Safety Tips

Always double-check the sender’s email, look for spelling mistakes, and never click on links or attachments from unknown sources.

Report suspicious emails to your organization’s IT department.
